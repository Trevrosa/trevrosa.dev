How can I help?

Diseases are one of the biggest threats to bees.


Over 30% of bee hives in the world experience Colony Collapse Disorder. Colony Collapse Disorder, or CCD, is when most worker bees leave the hive, and leave behind the queen.
There are many reasons for this, but one is the varroa mite (or the varroa destructor), which is a pest of honey bees. Varroa mites attach onto bees and suck their fat, which weakens them.
(You can see one of them here:)
Another reason is the parasite Nosema, which causes dysentery. It shows as yellow stripes on the outside of the hive and in when it's severe, inside the hive as well.
