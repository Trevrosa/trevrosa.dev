How can I help?

Habitat loss and pollution is a serious threat to pollination.

It's very important for bees to have enough flowers to pollinate, and safe places to use for creating hives.
But since World War 2, the world lost 97% of our wildflower meadows, leaving our bees with little natural habitat.

Because of the loss of vital habitats for agriculture, wildlife, especially bees, has become more reliant on protected wildlife sites.
Despite this, government records shows that only 6% of habitats protected by EU regulations in the UK are in good condition.


Also, pollution in the air can affect bees.
According to a recent study, air pollution makes it more difficult for bees and other insects to follow the scent of flowers, potentially limiting pollination by up to a third.
